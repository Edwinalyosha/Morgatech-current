Don’t Let Your Car Break Down: Identify These 7 Common Problems Early!

Maintaining your car can feel overwhelming. In this post, our expert mechanics share their knowledge about some of the most common car troubles, what they mean, and how they can affect your vehicle’s performance. Identifying these issues can help you keep your car running efficiently and save you money.

Dashboard warning lights

Dashboard warning lights are very important features in modern vehicles, your friend designed to alert you to potential issues before they become major problems.
The most common is the check engine light, which can signal a range of issues from a loose gas cap to more serious engine problems. But it's not just the check engine light you need to watch out for. The oil pressure light means your car is low on oil, and your engine is at a risk of damage. The battery warning light suggests an issue with your charging systemor battery. And the brake warning light? That's a sign to check your brake fluid levels or get your brakes inspected right away. If you see any of these warning lights, it's best to get your car checked by a professional mechanic to avoid further issues.

Sputtering engine

A sputtering engine can be caused by several issues, from a dirty fuel injector to a clogged air filter or even a failing spark plug. Ignoring this can lead to poor fuel economy, reduced performance, and even engine damage. Addressing a sputtering engine early can save you money and keep your car running smoothly. If you're experiencing engine trouble, don’t wait, schedule a diagnostic check today and ensure your vehicle stays in top condition.

Shaking steering wheel

A shaking steering wheel can be caused by unbalanced tires, misaligned wheels, or even worn-out suspension components. When your tires are out of balance, you'll often feel the shake in the steering wheel at higher speeds. Wheel alignment problems can make your car pull to one side and cause uneven tire wear, leading to vibration. If your steering wheel is shaking, get it checked by a professional mechanic as soon as you can. We provide an exhaustive array of services at Morgatech Auto to help you with such a situation

Brake system issues

Squeaking brakes usually mean that the brake pads need to be replaced. Ignoring this can lead to more serious problems, like damage to your brake rotors, which are much more expensive to fix. Besides squeaking, other brake issues to watch out for include a spongy brake pedal, which can indicate air in the brake lines or low brake fluid, and vibrations when braking. These brake problems can significantly impact your car’s safety and performance. If you’re experiencing any brake issues, schedule a brake inspection or brake pad replacement today to ensure your vehicle stays safe on the road.

Car fluid leakages

Leaking fluids are a common issue that can indicate various problems with your vehicle. Identifying the type of fluid leaking from your car can help diagnose the issue early and prevent more significant damage. Engine oil leaks are usually dark brown or black. Coolant leaks are typically green, yellow, or pink. Transmission fluid is usually red or brown. Brake fluid leaks are typically clear to light brown and can seriously affect your stopping power, it is absolutely important to deal with these as soon as you noyice. Spotting these leaks early and getting them checked by a professional mechanic can sometimes mean the difference between life and death.

Excessive vibrations when driving

Common causes of a rough ride include unbalanced or misaligned wheels, worn-out suspension components, or even tire problems. Ignoring these issues can lead to uneven tire wear, poor fuel efficiency, and more extensive damage to your car’s suspension system. If you’re feeling vibrations or experiencing a rough ride, it’s crucial to get your vehicle inspected by a professional mechanic.

Strange noises

Hearing strange noises while driving your car can be unsettling Different noises can indicate various issues. Our professional mechanics can take a look at your car and identify the issue, preventing more serious and costly repairs down the line. Dont ignore any sounds you car makes even if it only happens once in a while. Remember, a stitch in time saves nine.
